# Digital SAT — Structure & Mechanics Brief (current as of 2026)

The version below is the **digital, adaptive SAT** delivered in College Board's **Bluebook** app (digital internationally since March 2023, in the US since March 2024). All facts cross-checked across College Board official pages and reputable prep sources.

## 1. Section structure

The test has **two sections**, each split into **two equal-length modules**:

| Section | Modules | Questions / module | Total questions | Time / module | Section time |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Reading & Writing (R&W) | 2 | 27 | 54 | 32 min | 64 min |
| Math | 2 | 22 | 44 | 35 min | 70 min |
| **Total** | **4** | — | **98** | — | **134 min (2h14m)** |

- One **10-minute break** sits between the R&W section and the Math section.
- Total seated test time is **2 hours 14 minutes** (excluding the break and check-in).
- Sources: [College Board – How the SAT Is Structured](https://satsuite.collegeboard.org/sat/whats-on-the-test/structure); [Princeton Review – Digital SAT Structure](https://www.princetonreview.com/college-advice/digital-sat-structure); [EdisonOS – Digital SAT Format 2026](https://www.edisonos.com/digital-sat/structure-breakdown).

Within R&W, the four content domains are Craft & Structure, Information & Ideas, Standard English Conventions, and Expression of Ideas; within Math, Algebra, Advanced Math, Problem-Solving & Data Analysis, and Geometry & Trigonometry. R&W pairs **one short passage (~25–150 words) with a single question**; questions are ordered roughly easy→hard within each domain group.

## 2. Multistage adaptive testing (MST)

The SAT is **section-adaptive** (adaptive at the module level, *not* question-by-question), in two stages per section:

- **Module 1** of each section is the same for everyone: "a broad mix of easy, medium, and hard questions" with a **medium average difficulty**.
- Based on performance on Module 1, the test routes you to **one of two versions of Module 2**: an **easier** Module 2 (more easy/medium items) or a **harder** Module 2 (more hard items). Strong Module-1 performance → harder Module 2; weaker → easier Module 2.
- Your final section score "will reflect your achievement and skills based on your answers to questions in **both** modules."
- Sources: [College Board Blog – What Is Digital SAT Adaptive Testing](https://blog.collegeboard.org/what-digital-sat-adaptive-testing); [Applerouth – Complete Guide to the Digital Adaptive SAT](https://www.applerouth.com/blog/the-complete-guide-to-the-digital-adaptive-sat); [EdisonOS – How Adaptive Testing Works](https://www.edisonos.com/digital-sat/adaptive-testing).

### How routing caps your maximum score
The harder Module 2 contains the higher-value items needed to reach the top of the scale. Per College Board: **"If you get a couple questions wrong on the first module, scoring an 800 for the section won't be possible even if you get every answer right in the second module."** In practice, being routed to the **easier** Module 2 places a **ceiling well below 800** on that section regardless of how perfectly you answer it — so Module 1 accuracy is disproportionately important. To hit a 1600 you effectively must be routed to (and largely ace) both hard modules ([College Board Blog](https://blog.collegeboard.org/what-digital-sat-adaptive-testing)).

## 3. Question counts: scored vs. pretest (experimental)

**Each module contains 2 unscored "pretest" (experimental) questions.** They look identical to scored questions and are unidentifiable, so all must be attempted.

| | Total / module | Scored / module | Unscored / module |
|---|---|---|---|
| R&W module | 27 | 25 | 2 |
| Math module | 22 | 20 | 2 |

- **Scored items:** 50 R&W + 40 Math = **90 operational** scored questions.
- **Unscored items:** **8 total** (2 per module × 4 modules).
- Sources: [Test Innovators – Digital SAT Guide](https://testinnovators.com/blog/digital-sat-guide/); [Vibrant Publishers – Digital SAT](https://www.vibrantpublishers.com/blogs/blogs-on-act-sat/digital-sat-all-you-need-to-know-about-it).

Pace works out to roughly **71 seconds/question in R&W** (32 min ÷ 27) and **95 seconds/question in Math** (35 min ÷ 22) — notably more time per item than the paper SAT.

## 4. Scoring

- **Each section: 200–800.** **Composite: 400–1600** (R&W + Math). There is no separate essay (the optional essay was retired).
- The two section scores are summed for the total; there is no longer a combined "EBRW" section on the test itself, though percentile tables and older references still use "EBRW" interchangeably with R&W.
- **Raw-to-scaled behavior:** scoring uses **Item Response Theory (IRT)**, not a flat raw-point curve. Questions are **weighted by difficulty** and the difficulty of the module you were routed to is factored in, so a correct hard-module answer contributes more than a correct easy-module answer. This is why the **easy Module 2 caps the attainable scaled score** for that section. Concretely: a near-perfect raw performance routed through the hard modules maps to ~800/section, whereas the same raw count on easy modules maps to a substantially lower scaled score ([College Board Blog](https://blog.collegeboard.org/what-digital-sat-adaptive-testing); [Test Innovators](https://testinnovators.com/blog/digital-sat-guide/)).
- Scores are typically returned in **days** (often ~2–4 business days) via Bluebook/College Board account, much faster than the old ~2–3 week paper turnaround.

## 5. Calculator, reference sheet, Bluebook & devices

- **Calculator allowed throughout the entire Math section** (the old "no-calculator" math section is gone). **No calculator is permitted on R&W.**
- **Desmos graphing + scientific calculator is built into Bluebook** (toggle between the two); students may instead bring an **approved handheld calculator**. **CAS calculators are banned** (College Board no longer publishes an approved-models list but prohibits CAS, phones/smartwatches/tablets/laptops, and anything with internet/camera).
- A **formula/reference sheet is provided on-screen in the Math section** (geometry formulas, etc.).
- Source: [College Board – SAT Calculator Policy](https://satsuite.collegeboard.org/sat/what-to-bring-do/calculator-policy); [OnePrep – SAT Calculator Policy 2026](https://www.oneprep.co/blog/sat-calculator-policy-2026-bluebook-desmos-rules).
- **Bluebook** is College Board's free testing app. Students test on a **personal laptop/tablet** (Windows, Mac, iPad, or school-managed Chromebook), or **borrow a school/College Board-provided device**. Bluebook handles timing, the embedded Desmos calculator, reference sheet, annotation/flagging tools, and even short connectivity drops (answers are saved locally and submitted when reconnected).

## 6. What changed vs. the old paper SAT

| Feature | Old paper SAT | Current digital SAT |
|---|---|---|
| Length | ~3 hrs (+ breaks) | **~2 hr 14 min** |
| Sections | 4 (Reading, Writing&Language, Math No-Calc, Math Calc) | **2 (R&W, Math)** |
| Total questions | 154 | **98** |
| Format | Linear/fixed paper form | **Adaptive, two-stage, in Bluebook** |
| Reading passages | Long (500–750 words), multiple Qs each | **Short (~25–150 words), one Q each** |
| Calculator | Banned on one Math section | **Allowed throughout Math; Desmos built in** |
| Time per question | Less | **More** (e.g., ~1.2–1.6 min) |
| Scoring speed | ~2–3 weeks | **~days** |

Score scale (400–1600) and section ranges (200–800) are **unchanged**. Sources: [Magoosh – New Digital SAT Format](https://magoosh.com/sat/new-digital-sat-format/); [Target Test Prep – Digital vs Paper SAT](https://sat.blog.targettestprep.com/digital-sat-vs-paper-sat/); [PrepMatters – Digital vs Paper SAT](https://prepmatters.com/blog/sat-act/digital-sat-vs-paper-sat/).

Percentiles below are based on recent test-takers (College Board reports both **User** percentiles — among SAT takers — and **Nationally Representative** percentiles — among all US 11th/12th graders, which run higher). The averages and totals are cross-checked between PrepScholar and Magoosh.

**Total composite:**
| Total | ~Percentile |
|---|---|
| 1600 | 99+ |
| 1550–1600 | 99+ |
| 1520 | ~99 (top ~1%) |
| 1500 | ~98 |
| 1400 | ~93 |
| 1200 | ~76 |
| ~1050 | ~50–56 (**national average ≈ 1050**) |

**Section scores (User percentiles):**
| Score | Math | Reading & Writing |
|---|---|---|
| 800 | 99–99+ | 99+ |
| 700 | ~92–93 | ~93–95 |
| 650 | ~84–87 | ~83–87 |

- A **700 R&W** sits around the **93rd–95th** percentile; a **700 Math** around the **92nd–93rd**. An **800 Math** is ~99th (Math has slightly more perfect scores, so 800 Math is marginally "easier" percentile-wise than 800 R&W, which is 99+). A **1500** total ≈ **98th** percentile; a **1600** is **99+** (top fraction of a percent).
- Average section scores ≈ **~530 R&W / ~520 Math** (≈1050 total).
- Sources: [PrepScholar – SAT Percentiles 2025](https://blog.prepscholar.com/sat-percentiles-and-score-rankings); [Magoosh – Good/Average SAT Scores 2025–2026](https://magoosh.com/sat/sat-scores/); [College Board Research – SAT Percentiles](https://research.collegeboard.org/reports/sat-suite/understanding-scores/sat).

## 8. Test dates / frequency

- In the US the SAT is offered on **weekend (Saturday) dates roughly 7 times per academic year** — traditionally **August, October, November, December, March, May, June**, with College Board having added a **September** administration in recent cycles (so some published 2025–26/2026–27 calendars list 8 weekend dates). There is **no test in January, February, April, or July**.
- **Published 2025–26 weekend dates** (per search of current calendars): March 14, May 2, June 6, **and** the 2026 fall sittings Aug 22, Sep 12, Oct 3, Nov 7, Dec 5, 2026.
- **2026–27** follows the same monthly pattern (one Saturday each in Aug, Sep, Oct, Nov, Dec, Mar, May, Jun); early-published 2027 spring dates include **March 6, May 1, June 5, 2027**.
- Separately, **SAT School Day** administrations run on **weekdays** during the school year (offered through participating schools/states), independent of the weekend dates.
- Registration: regular deadline ~3 weeks before test day; late registration ~11–12 days before (standard fee + ~$38 late fee).
- Sources: [College Board – SAT Dates & Deadlines](https://satsuite.collegeboard.org/sat/dates-deadlines); [Princeton Review – 2026-2027 SAT Test Dates](https://www.princetonreview.com/college/sat-test-dates); [PrepScholar – Future SAT Test Dates](https://blog.prepscholar.com/future-years-sat-test-dates-schedule-and-registration-deadlines).

### Quick-reference summary
- **2 sections × 2 modules; 98 questions; 2h14m; one 10-min break.** R&W = 54 Q / 64 min (27 Q × 32 min); Math = 44 Q / 70 min (22 Q × 35 min).
- **2 pretest (unscored) questions per module → 8 total**; 90 scored (50 R&W + 40 Math).
- **Adaptive**: Module 1 (medium) routes to easy/hard Module 2; routing **caps** the section score — miss items in Module 1 and 800 becomes unreachable.
- **200–800/section, 400–1600 total**, IRT-weighted by difficulty/route.
- **Desmos calculator + reference sheet built into Bluebook; calculator allowed all of Math, none on R&W; no CAS.** Test on own or school device.
- **2026 percentiles**: 1600 = 99+, 1500 ≈ 98th, average ≈ 1050; 700 R&W ≈ 93–95th, 800 Math ≈ 99th.
